### 86. The Various Motions of the Air-Aether Particles Make Them Perfectly Spherical

The fire-aether coming from vortices like KL and similar ones is primarily carried towards the Sun. But many parts of it are also dispersed throughout the entire vortex AYBM. From there it transitions to other vortices like CO and similar ones. 

By flowing around the particles of the air-aether, it causes them to move not only around their own centers but also possibly in other ways. 

These particles are thus agitated in many different ways simultaneously. Whatever their shape was initially, they must now be perfectly spherical, not like a cylinder or any spheroid that is round in only one part.


### 87. The Various Degrees of Speed in the Minute Parts of the First Element

After explaining the nature of the first and second elements to the extent necessary to discuss the third, we must consider that the matter of the first element is not equally agitated in all its minute parts, but often in a very small quantity, numerous different degrees of speed are found. 

This can be easily demonstrated both from the manner in which its generation was described above and from its continuous use: for we assumed it to be generated from the fact that the particles of the second element, not yet spherical but angular and filling the entire space in which they were, could not move without their angles being worn down, and the minute parts separated from them by this friction changed their shapes in various ways according to the different spaces they occupied, thus assuming the form of the first element; and now we still suppose that the first element serves to fill all the narrow spaces found around other bodies. 

Hence, it is clear that each of its minute parts was initially no larger than the angles of the particles from which they were cut, or than the space left in the middle by three mutually touching globules; and therefore some of them could have remained perfectly undivided, while others, meanwhile, exiting from narrow spaces whose shape was changing more and more, had to be indefinitely divided. 

For example, let there be three globules ABC, two of which, A and B, mutually touching at G, rotate only around their own centers, while the third, C, touching the first at E, rolls over it from E to I until it touches the second at point F; it is clear that the matter of the first element contained in the triangular space FGI, whether consisting of several fragments or just one, can meanwhile remain immobile; but the matter in the space FIED must necessarily move, and no fragment so small can be designated between points D and F that is not larger than the one removed from there at each moment. Because the globule C, approaching B, causes the line DF to pass through innumerable different degrees of brevity.



### 88. Those Minute Parts Which Have the Least Speed Easily Transfer What They Have to Others and Stick Together

Thus, in the matter of the first element, some fragments are less divided and less swiftly agitated than others; since they are supposed to have been cut from the angles of the particles of the second element when they were not yet turned into globules and filled all the spaces alone, they must necessarily have very angular shapes and be unsuited for motion.

Hence, they easily stick together and transfer much of their agitation to those other fragments which are the smallest and most swiftly agitated: because, according to the laws of nature, larger bodies more easily transfer their agitation to smaller ones, other things being equal, than receive any new agitation from those others.


### 89. Such Sticking Minute Parts Are Mainly Found in the Matter of the First Element, Which Moves from the Poles to the Centers of the Vortices

Indeed, such fragments are mainly found in the matter of the first element, which moves from the poles towards the center of the sky in straight lines: for its least agitated parts suffice for this straight motion, but not for other more oblique and varied motions occurring in other places; from which they are therefore usually expelled into the path of this straight motion; and there they gather into small masses, whose shape I want to consider carefully here.


### 90. The Shape of These Minute Parts, Which Will Be Called Striated Particles

Since they often pass through those narrow triangular spaces found in the middle of three mutually touching globules of the second element, they must acquire a shape that is triangular in its width and depth. 

However, as for their length, it is not easy to determine, since it does not seem to depend on any other cause than the amount of matter from which these small masses are formed; but it suffices to conceive them as small columns, excavated with three striations twisted like spirals, so that they can pass by rotating through those narrow passages, having the shape of the curvilinear triangle FGI, always found between three mutually touching globules of the second element. 

Since they are elongated and move very swiftly among these globules of the second element, while the latter rotate around the poles of the sky with another motion, it is clear that their striations must be twisted like spirals; and indeed more or less twisted, depending on whether they pass through parts closer or further from the axis of the vortex; because the globules of the second element rotate faster in those than in these, as mentioned earlier.


### 91. These Particles Coming from Opposite Poles Are Twisted in Contrary Directions

Also, since they come towards the middle of the sky from opposite parts; some from the South Pole, others from the North Pole, while the whole vortex moves around its axis in one and the same direction, it is clear that those coming from the South Pole must be twisted in opposite directions to those coming from the North Pole. This is very noteworthy because the forces of the magnet, which will be explained below, mainly depend on this.
