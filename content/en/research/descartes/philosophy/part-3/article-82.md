### 82. The Smaller Size and Faster Movement of Air-Aether Near the Sun, Compared to Those Further Away, Up to a Certain Distance Beyond Which All Are Equal in Size and Move Faster the Further They Are from the Sun

Those air-aether closest to the center of each vortex are smaller and move faster than those slightly farther away. This goes on up to a certain limit. 


Beyond this limit, the outer ones move faster than the inner ones. 

In terms of size, they are all equal. 

<!-- sky -->
For example, in the first gravitational territory, the smallest air-aether particles are near the surface of the Sun at `defg`, and slightly further away.

They gradually increase in size up to the surface of the spheroid `HNQR`, beyond which all are equal in size. 

Those on this surface HNQR move the slowest of all, so that perhaps the particles at HQ take thirty years or even more to complete one circuit around the poles AB, while those above towards M and Y, and below towards e and g, move faster, completing their circuits within a few weeks.


### 83. Why the Most Distant Particles Move Faster Than Those Slightly Closer

First, the upper ones towards `M` and `Y` must move faster than the lower ones towards H and Q. 

All were initially equal in size (as was fitting since we had no evidence of their inequality).

The space in which they are circularly moved like in a vortex is not precisely round partly because surrounding vortices are not equal, and partly because it must be narrower near the center of each of these adjacent vortices than in other parts; 

It is necessary that sometimes some move faster than others when they must change order to pass from a wider to a narrower path. 

For example, two spheres between points A and B cannot pass between two closer points C and D unless one precedes the other, and it is evident that the one which precedes moves faster. 

Furthermore, since all particles of the first sky try with all their might to recede from the center S, as soon as one of them moves faster than its neighbors, it immediately recedes more from that center, and thus it is always the upper ones that move faster. 

However, the speed of their movement can only be learned through experience. 

We have no experience of it except in comets, which I will show below migrate from one sky to another.

Nor can we determine the slowness of the circle HQ except from the motion of Saturn, which I will demonstrate is within or below it.


### 84. Why Those Nearest the Sun Also Move Faster Than Those Slightly Further Away

The circumvolution of solar matter sweeps all the adjacent part of the sky along with it.

Within the limit `HQ`, the particles closer to the center `S` complete their circuit faster than those farther away. 

It is agitated very quickly and always emits some of itself through narrow passages among the air-aether particles towards the ecliptic and receives it towards the poles.

This is why it has the power to sweep these particles along with it up to a certain distance. 

We designate the limit of this distance with the ellipse HNQR, not a circle. 

Although the Sun is spherical and does not propel the sky's matter surrounding it with less force towards the poles than towards the ecliptic, in that action in which we said its light consists, the same cannot be understood of this other action, by which it sweeps the sky's matter along with it in orbit because it depends solely on its circular motion around its axis.

This motion is stronger in the ecliptic than towards the poles. Therefore, `H` and `Q` must be further from `S` than `N` and `R`.

Hence, the reason will be given below why the tails of comets sometimes appear straight and sometimes curved.


### 85. Why Those Near the Sun Are Smaller Than Those Farther Away

<!-- celestial matter -->
Within the limit HQ, the lower particles of air-aether move faster than the upper ones, they must also be smaller.

If they were larger or equal, they would thereby have more force and thus would become the upper ones. 

But once it happens that one is much smaller than those above it so that it is more surpassed in size than it surpasses them in speed, it must always remain lower than them. 

Even if we suppose that these particles were made as accurately equal as possible by God at the beginning, it could not happen over time, due to the inequality of the spaces they traverse and the inequality of their motions arising from that, as demonstrated a little earlier, that some would not become smaller than others, and there must be enough of these smaller particles to fill the space HNQR. 

We do not compare this space to the size of the entire vortex AYBM except as a very small part; so too must the size of the Sun be understood as very small compared to it; although this proportion could not be shown in the figure here because it would have to be too vast.

There are various other inequalities in the motions of parts of the sky, especially those between `S` and `H` or `Q`.
