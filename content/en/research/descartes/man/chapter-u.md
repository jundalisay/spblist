Another effect is seen; that when the gland is thus held inclined in some way, it is hindered from receiving ideas of objects that affect the organs of other senses so easily. As here, for example, when almost all the spirits produced by gland H emanate from points a, b, c, they do not sufficiently emanate from point d to form the idea of object D. Hence I conclude that the action is not as lively, nor as effective, as from a, b, c. From which it is evident that the ideas interfere with each other: and therefore, the more senses are involved, the weaker is each one individually. It must also be noted that whenever the organs of the senses are affected more strongly by this than by that, not yet accurately disposed to receive that action, the presence of that object alone is sufficient to complete that disposition. So, if the eye, for example, is arranged to look at a very remote place, when perhaps the object A, B, C, which is very close to it, begins to present itself: it is certain that the action of this object can cause it to be immediately arranged to be looked at with a fixed gaze.






32. Finally, let us trace the spirits into the nerves and consider the motions dependent on them. If, in the innermost part of the brain, some of the tubes are not more open or in a different manner than others, and consequently, if the particular spirits do not have the impression of a certain idea, they are dispersed haphazardly and indifferently in all directions and pass through the pores around B into those around C. 

Hence, the finer particles of them will flow out completely through the pores of the membrane that envelops it, while the remaining parts of the spirits flowing towards D will pass into the nerves and muscles without producing any particular effect in them, as they are evenly distributed to all. 

However, if some of those tubes, due to the action of the object on the sensing agent, are more or less open, or are opened differently than their neighbors, the filaments composing the substance of the brain (therefore some being slightly more intense or relaxed than others) will lead the spirits to a certain part of their base, and from there into certain nerves with greater or lesser force than others. This is sufficient to stimulate different movements in the muscles, as explained more extensively above.
