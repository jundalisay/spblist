{16:1} Sarai, Abram’s wife, bare him no children. She had an Egyptian servant named Hagar.

{16:2} Sarai told Abram:

Enki had restrained me from bearing: Please have sex with my maid so you can have a child by her.

Abram hearkened to the voice of Sarai. 

{16:3} Sarai took Hagar her maid the Egyptian, after
Abram had dwelt ten years in the land of Canaan, and gave
her to her husband Abram to be his wife.

{16:4} He had sex with Hagar and she conceived. Sarai then despised Hagar. 

{16:5} Sarai said to Abram:

I made a mistake. I have given my maid to you. When she saw that she had conceived, she despised me. May Enki judge between you and me.

{16:6} But Abram said unto Sarai, Your servant is in your hand. Do whatever you want with her. And when Sarai dealt hardly with her, she fled from her face.

{16:7} A worker for Enki found her by a fountain of water in the wilderness, by the fountain in the
way to Shur. {16:8} And he said,
